Other Pertinent Information

At Target, I have worked in the Warehousing department the whole time. The basics of how the Warehouse works is that Inbound receives product, Outbound ships it to the stores, and Warehouse handles everything between. This includes pulling product off of stored pallets, putting pallets in the racking, pulling pallets out of the racking, and transporting pallets and carts of product from various locations to their destinations.

During my time at Target, I was a part of the setup of the Inventory Control and Quality Assurance team. Their primary goal is to look through audit trails when defects (such as errors in counts or product IDs) occurred and find the root cause of the defect in an attempt to develop new processes and/or procedures to prevent the same defects from happening in the future. This meant developing tools that would go through the Inventory Management System history and pull the data in a searchable format. This was done by me and two other colleges using Excel macros (VBA) to make input fields and query the Database and then format the output in a format that could be used by team members and management to see the history of various things, such as pallet IDs, locations, product IDs (DPCIs), team member IDs, etc.

I am also a part of the Emergency Response Team (ERT). A counterpart of the safety team, the ERT is trained and certificated by the American Red Cross in first aid, CPR, and AED use. The team is lead by an on-site Registered Nurse who conducts weekly trainings on more advanced first aid topics tailored to Warehouse incidents. We respond to injuries and illness both minor and severe and assess the person and situation. We determine the best course of action, which sometimes means calling Emergency Medical Personnel. If EMT is called, we initiate a Code Green, which stops production, instructs all team members to removed powered equipment from travel aisles and stop operating equipment, and all ERT personnel to the site of the incident. This also give EMT a clear path to the injured team member.
